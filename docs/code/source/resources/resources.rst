=========
Resources
=========

Resources are a fundamental part of the game simulation.

Cities both produce and consume resources. A city's size is affected by the rate of resource production and consumption.

An important part of the game simulation is the supply chain of resources between cities.

In order to grow meaningfully, a city must be able to "sell" the resources it produces. A city which cannot sell its resources will 
may eventually shrink or die.

Likewise, a city must be able to "buy" the resources it needs.

While resource categories are not strictly defined, major categories of resources are:

.. _raw-goods:

Raw Goods
=========

These are the resources that cities produce from the land itself. They are the most basic resources, and they are necessary 
for the production of more complex goods. Some cities may be able to produce different or higher quality raw goods than others, 
depending on the region they are in. Its important that cities lacking in certain raw goods are able to "buy" them from other 
cities that can produce them.

  * Wheat
  * Wood
  * Stone
  * Cotton

Basic Goods
===========

These are "nice-to-have" resources that are produced from raw goods. They are not strictly necessary for a city to grow, 
but they can help a city grow faster operate more efficiently. They are also necessary for the production of more complex goods.

  * Bread
  * Paper
  * Furniture
  * Clothing
  * Hand Tools

Industrial Goods
================

Industrial goods are the product of industry. They are more complex, and more valuable than basic goods.

  * Iron
  * Tools
  * Chemicals

Technology Goods
================

Technology goods are more complex and more valuable than industrial goods. They are the product of advanced industry,
and they are necessary for the production of luxury goods.

  * Circuits
  * Microchips
  * Processors

Automation Goods
================

Automation goods are the most complex and most valuable goods. They are the product of advanced industry and technology.
They have the potential to greatly increase a city's growth and efficiency, but they are also the most difficult to produce.

  * Robots
  * Vision Systems
  * CNC Machines
  * 3D Printers
  * Laser Cutters
  * Server Farms

Transportation Goods
====================

Transportation goods are a special category of goods that are necessary for the transportation of other goods.

  * Trains
  * Tracks
